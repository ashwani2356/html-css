OUR MUZAFFARPUR❤️

Muzaffarpur, known as the "Lychee Kingdom of India," is a major city in North Bihar famous for its cultural landmarks, temples, and bustling markets. Key attractions include the revered Baba Garibnath Mandir, the scenic Jubba Sahni Park, and the historic Ramchandra Shahi Sangrahalaya.Founded in the 18th century and named after Muzaffar Khan, it is a significant commercial and educational hub in North Bihar. The city serves as a key transit point connecting to Nepal

Litchi Gardens

Litchi Garden is a popular tourist destination located in Muzaffarpur, Bihar, India. It is known for its vast expanse of litchi orchards and is one of the largest litchi gardens in the world. The garden covers an area of over 200 acres and is home to over 50,000 litchi trees.

Garib Sthan Mandir

Garib Sthan Mandir is a famous religious site located in the city of Muzaffarpur in the state of Bihar, India. The temple is dedicated to the Sufi saint Hazrat Makhdoom Syed Shah Ruknuddin Alam, who is also known as Shah Baba by the locals

Jubba Sahni Park

Jubba Sahni Park is a well:known public park located in the Kadam Kuan area of Patna, Bihar, India. The park is named after Jubba Sahni, a freedom fighter and social worker who played an important role in India's independence movement.

Devi Mandir

Devi Mandir in Muzaffarpur is dedicated to Goddess Durga and is one of the 51 Shaktipeeth and is also known as Maa Raj Rajeshwari Mandir.You can expect a large footfall of devotees, especially around Navratri and Durga Pooja time.

Motijheel

Motijheel was built during the British colonial period and was originally a water reservoir used for irrigation purposes. It was later converted into a public park and is now a popular tourist destination.

Saraiyaganj Tower

Saraiyaganj Tower, located in the heart of Muzaffarpur, Bihar (842001), is an iconic 40-foot historical watchtower established in 1952 to honor freedom fighters. Inaugurated by Dr. Rajendra Prasad, it serves as a central landmark and Shahid Smarak for North Bihar.

Sahu pokhar

Sahu Pokhar Mandir is a 250-year-old, historically significant Hindu temple complex located near Purani Bazar in Muzaffarpur, Bihar, known for its centrally located large pond ("pokhar") and Vishnu Panchayatana shrine.

Lahthi market

The Islampur Market in Muzaffarpur is globally renowned for its Lahthi (lac bangles), which are a vibrant symbol of Bihar's rich cultural heritage. Locally referred to as the Lahthi Market, this bustling commercial hub is the heart of the city's traditional bangle-making industry.
